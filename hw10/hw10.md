## P1
The constant solution $u(x,t)\equiv 1$ does not satisfy the assumed homogeneous Dirichlet boundary condition, since
$u(0,t)=u(1,t)=1 \neq 0$.

Therefore Poincar√©'s inequality (which requires homogeneous Dirichlet boundary conditions) is not applicable, the differential inequality for $E(t)$ used to prove (2) is not valid, and hence the energy estimate (2) need not (and in general does not) hold for this solution.

An exact solution with homogeneous Dirichlet boundary conditions is $u(x,t) = \sin(\pi x)\,e^{-\nu \pi^2 t}$.

Indeed, $u_t = -\nu\pi^2 \sin(\pi x)e^{-\nu\pi^2 t}, \quad u_{xx} = -\pi^2 \sin(\pi x)e^{-\nu\pi^2 t}$.

So $u_t = \nu u_{xx}$, and $u(0,t)=u(1,t)=0$.
